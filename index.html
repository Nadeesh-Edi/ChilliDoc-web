Literature Survey

Project Scope

Identifying chili plant disease using image processing

Image processing is a domain that holds enormous potential for the agriculture sector, especially in disease detection. Chili plants are susceptible to various diseases, which can significantly impact yield and quality. Timely and accurate identification of these diseases is crucial for effective management. Here, we review several methods and studies that have used image processing for chili plant disease identification.
1. Traditional Image Processing Techniques.
a. Color-Based Detection: Rokade & Vibhute [9] used color features in image processing to detect diseases in chili plants. This method primarily focuses on analyzing the color variations in leaf images to detect anomalies. While this method can be effective in some cases, it is often not sufficient as diseases can also affect texture. b. Texture and Shape Analysis: Along with color, texture and shape are also important characteristics that change due to disease. Anami & Savakar [5] used texture features to classify healthy and diseased chili leaves. c. Thresholding and Clustering: These are classical image processing methods where the image is segmented based on pixel intensity. Thresholding can help in focusing on regions of interest, such as spots on leaves.
2. Deep Learning-Based Image Processing Techniques.
a. Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs): These are advanced deep learning models specifically designed for image processing. Ahsan [10] used a CNN model for detecting Capsicum leaf diseases. They used transfer learning by fine-tuning pre-trained models on a custom dataset of Capsicum leaf images. b. Data Augmentation and Active Learning: Deep learning models, especially CNNs, require a large amount of data. Dhaka et al. [19] used a relatively small dataset for detecting chili plant diseases, which may not be sufficient. Data augmentation techniques such as rotation, flipping, and scaling can artificially expand the dataset. Active learning can also be used to train models more efficiently by smartly querying the most informative data points.
3. Hybrid Techniques
a. Integration of Classical and Deep Learning Methods: Hybrid models that integrate classical image processing techniques with deep learning models can be developed for better performance [21]. Classical methods can be used for preprocessing and feature extraction, while deep learning models can be used for classification. b. Combining Multiple Models for Improved Accuracy: Combining the outputs of multiple models, each trained with different characteristics, can sometimes lead to better performance [20].

Identifying corrective measures for diseases, preventive methods, and ideal conditions for optimal growth

Disease management and fostering the ideal growing conditions are critical for chili production. Several research studies have been undertaken to explore corrective measures for diseases, preventive methods, and optimal growth conditions.
a. Biological Control Agents: Anjum et al. [1] investigated the use of Trichoderma spp. as a biological control agent against Phytophthora capsici, which causes fruit to rot in chili plants. The study concluded that Trichoderma spp. showed strong antifungal activity, suggesting that it could be a viable alternative to chemical fungicides. Similarly, Gashua et al. [2] found that the colonization of chili plants by arbuscular mycorrhizal fungi (AMF) not only enhanced nutrient uptake and growth but also bolstered resistance to diseases like Fusarium wilt and root rot.
b. Integrated Pest Management (IPM): Eliyatiningsih et al. [4] emphasized the use of Integrated Pest Management, which combines cultural, physical, and biological controls to effectively manage pests and diseases.
c. Nutrient Management: Ensuring that chili plants have access to the necessary nutrients is essential. Gashua et al. [2] highlighted how it helps in improving nutrient uptake. Farmers should ensure a balanced supply of macro and micronutrients to support optimal growth.
d. Optimal Growing Conditions: Temperature, light intensity, and humidity are among the environmental factors affecting the growth of chili plants. According to Gevens et al. [11], managing these factors is crucial. Farmers should monitor environmental conditions and, if necessary, use greenhouse cultivation to maintain optimal conditions.

Identifying chili plant growth stages and predicting harvest date using machine learning

Accurate identification of the growth stages of chili plants and the prediction of their harvest dates are essential for optimal crop management. Machine learning (ML) offers a set of powerful tools for automating these tasks by learning patterns from data.
a. Image-Based Identification using Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs): CNNs have been widely used in agriculture for image recognition. These deep learning models can be trained on labeled images of chili plants at various stages of growth.
b. Time-Series Analysis for Growth Trend Identification: Algorithms like ARIMA and LSTM can be used to analyze time-series data of chili plant growth [17]. By tracking metrics such as plant height, leaf count, and flowering over time, these models can identify patterns associated with different growth stages.
c. Decision Trees and Random Forests for Stage Classification: These algorithms can classify the growth stage based on various features such as leaf color, size, and the number of flowers. These models are interpretable and can provide insights into which features are most indicative of specific growth stages [22].
d. Regression Models for Harvest Date Prediction: Linear regression, polynomial regression, or more complex models like support vector regression can be used to predict when the chili plants will be ready for harvest based on historical data and growth metrics [16].
e. Utilizing Environmental Data: Incorporating external data such as temperature, humidity, and rainfall into machine learning models can improve accuracy in harvest date predictions. Putra et al. [18] developed a growth prediction model for red chili plants using environmental factors to train a regression model.

Predicting weekly future prices for both types of chilies by taking the exchange rate as an external factor.

Accurate price prediction for agricultural commodities, including chili, is crucial for farmers and traders to make informed decisions regarding selling or storing their produce. Predicting the weekly future prices for chili is particularly challenging due to the volatile nature of agricultural markets.
a. Artificial Neural Networks (ANNs): Jayawardena et al. [13] demonstrated the effectiveness of Artificial Neural Networks in predicting chili prices in Sri Lanka. By considering factors such as weather conditions, global commodity prices, and historical price data, ANNs have been shown to make accurate predictions.
b. Time Series Models: Besides ANNs, other time series models like ARIMA and Holt-Winters have been compared for chili price prediction by Jayawardena et al. [13] Though ANN outperformed these models, they still hold value in understanding the time-dependent structure of the data, which is important for weekly predictions.
c. Bayesian Networks: Nuvaisiyah et al. [12] used Bayesian Networks to predict chili prices using historical price and weather data. Bayesian Networks can be particularly useful in understanding the probabilistic relationships between various factors, including exchange rates, and their impact on chili prices.
d. Impact on Import and Export: Exchange rates directly impact the cost of importing and exporting goods. For chilies, a change in exchange rates can affect the international competitiveness of the product. This, in turn, affects supply and demand dynamics and can have a significant influence on prices.

Research Objectives

Identifying corrective measures for diseases, preventive methods and ideal conditions for optimal growth

Develop a system which identifies the agrochemicals and fertilizers for optimal chili growth using machine learning algorithms.

Identifying chili plant disease using image processing

Develop a chili plant disease identification system using image processing and machine learning techniques, particularly the MobileNet CNN model paired with the DWT-GLCM hybrid model.

Identify chili plant growth stages and predict harvest date

Develop a deep learning model to predict chili plant growth stages and harvest dates based on the collected dataset.

Predicting weekly future price for both types of chilli by taking exchange rate as an external factor.

Give accurate prediction of the retail price of both green chilli and capsicum

Methodology

A. Identifying chili plant diseases, growth stage, harvest date, weekly prices, and corrective measures.

a. Data Collection In the initial phase of this research, a comprehensive dataset was collected from a variety of sources to include a broad spectrum of information on chili plant diseases, agrochemicals, prices, exchange rates, harvest information and fertilizers. These sources included scholarly research papers, online databases, and insights from agricultural experts and specialists at CIC Pelwehera. The collected dataset comprised variables such as the types and amounts of agrochemicals and fertilizers applied, and the symptoms of diseases observed in chili plants. b. Data Pre-processing After data collection, the preprocessing phase was initiated to ensure the quality and consistency of the data. This involved a series of steps including data cleaning, transformation, and feature engineering to prepare the data for training machine learning models. Moreover, specialized techniques like outlier detection and normalization were employed to refine the data. The rebalancing techniques were also implemented during this phase to manage the skewed data and enhance the accuracy levels of the predictive models. c. Model Development In this stage, machine learning models were developed to identify the factors that influence chili growth stage, price, disease and to determine the quantity of fertilizer and agrochemicals that needs to be used. The dataset prepared in the pre-processing stage was utilized to train the models. Active learning is used to lessen the burden of unlabeled data.

B. Mobile Application Development

After the development of machine learning models, a mobile application was developed to aid farmers in making informed decisions for sustainable and efficient chili cultivation. The application, developed using Flutter, was integrated with the machine learning models. It was designed to be user-friendly and to provide recommendations on the optimal types and amounts of agrochemicals and fertilizers, and in identifying symptoms of chili diseases, thus enabling timely corrective actions. When a farmer uploads a picture of a plant suspected of being infected, the model analyses the picture and provides the disease description the plant is infected with and the stage of the identified disease. Several tests were conducted to ascertain that the developed application met the required standards in terms of functionality, usability, and practicality. These included Unit Testing, Integration Testing, System Testing, and User Acceptance Testing. These rigorous testing protocols were essential to guarantee the reliability, accuracy, and value of the system for the chili farmers.

C. Data Visualization and Analysis

The final phase involved the utilization of data visualization techniques to present the recommendations generated by the machine learning models in a user-friendly manner. This allowed chili farmers to easily comprehend and implement the advised cultivation practices. Additionally, the data generated by the system was thoroughly analyzed to glean insights into chili plant disease corrections and their management. This involved using data visualization to discern patterns and trends, and statistical analysis to evaluate the system’s effectiveness in enhancing chili plant health and diminishing disease incidence.

Research Problem & Solution

The automation of a poultry farm poses several challenges that need to be addressed for efficient operations. The research problem can be divided into three main areas: predicting egg production, monitoring farm conditions, and detecting unauthorized access. The first research problem involves accurately predicting egg production. It requires identifying the key predictors that significantly influence egg production and developing a reliable machine learning model. Factors such as environmental conditions, feed quality, lighting, and other relevant parameters need to be considered. The challenge is to gather and preprocess relevant data, and train a model that can provide accurate predictions of egg production. The second research problem is monitoring the farm conditions in real-time. This involves continuously collecting data on temperature, humidity, ventilation, water quality, lighting, and other parameters. The challenge is to design an integrated system that collects live condition data and provides timely alerts to the farm owner or manager in case of any deviations or anomalies. The third research problem is detecting and preventing unauthorized access to the poultry farm. Unauthorized entry poses risks to the poultry and the farm infrastructure. The challenge is to develop a robust system that can detect unauthorized access attempts and prevent entry by unauthorized individuals. Solution: To address the research problems, the solution involves implementing a combination of technologies. For predicting egg production, historical data on egg production and relevant factors can be collected and used to train a machine learning model. This model can then provide predictions based on the current conditions and inputs. For monitoring farm conditions, IoT devices and sensors can be deployed throughout the farm to collect real-time data on temperature, humidity, ventilation, water quality, and lighting. This data can be processed and analyzed by a central monitoring system, which can generate alerts and notifications for any abnormal conditions. To tackle unauthorized access, security measures such as surveillance cameras, access control systems, and biometric authentication can be implemented. These systems can detect and prevent unauthorized individuals from entering the farm premises. Additionally, creating an IoT device for collecting eggs can involve using sensors or smart devices within the egg-laying area to automatically detect and collect eggs. This data can be combined with other live updates on egg production to provide accurate information to the farm owner or manager. Overall, by leveraging machine learning, IoT devices, and security systems, the poultry farm can be automated to optimize egg production, monitor farm conditions, prevent unauthorized access, and provide real-time updates to the farm owner or manager.
